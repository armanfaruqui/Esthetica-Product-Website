why it works

The internet and social media have revolutionized how we experience and share information, ideas, inspiration, and art. Whether it's through sharing a painting on Instagram or a video on Tiktok, we can now express ourselves and connect with others in ways that were once unimaginable. Esthetica aims to further push our ability and eagerness to share our multi-dimensional ideas, experiences, and creativity. But what seperates it from being just another moodboard app?

Shareability

More information is communicated through Instagram stories , Facebook reels, and Tiktok videos than through books and articles amongst younger generations. This layout of what Esthetica outputs will be recognizeable, and ideal to fill the screen and captures user's attention. User's can easily set a song from the playlist to play alongside it. Even though a lot of what needs to be communicated can be present in the form of an expandable list, video, or 3D object, a hyperlink can always redirect users to the app where they can use the interactable elements.

Accessibility

Those who have the knowledge of how to use relatively complex tools within the realms of programming and the adobe suite can express their ideas in ways that facilitate a variety of mediums. However, not everyone is or necessarily wants to take the time to learn or apply these skills in order to communicate ideas that would otherwise require it. Apps that increase the accessibility the creation process of these skills are only growing in popularity. Tiktok vulgarizes the process of video editing and VFX to allow more users to create videos that were previously only possible with Premier Pro and After Effects. Instagram makes the process of applying complex filters and vignettes to images super simple without the need for Photoshop.

Music

Streaming services, especially Spotify, have contributed to the significance of music taste and preferences within our global culture. Most subcultures can be identified and categorized based on their general taste in musical artists and genres. The Spotify playlist at the bottom is one of the main differentiating factors. Immediately upon the loading of an 'esthetic' a specified song from that playlist accompanies the user setting the mood as they eyes begin to explore the tiles. User's can then expand the playlist to view all of the songs, and obtain a link to listen to that playlist later. The other visual elements that accompany the playlist can help accustom user's palette to sounds they are not used to by creating an abstract context to associate with the music.
